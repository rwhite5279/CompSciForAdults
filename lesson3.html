  2. Algorithms
    • Peanut Butter and Jelly sandwiches

      Making a sandwich

      A classic exercise that introduces one to the idea of a "series of steps used to solve a problem," or algorithm, is this one: "How would you give instructions to someone to make a peanut butter and jelly sandwich?"

      Well? How would you?!

    • Calculating Sums

      Add the numbers from 1 to 100

      One algorithm for find the sum of the numbers from 1 to 100 is to add them up: 1 + 2 = 3, and 3 + 4 = 7, and 7 + 5 = 12, ...

      Identify another algorithm that arrives at the answer more quickly.

    • Counting Words

      Count the number of words in a text

      It's easy for people to count words in a phrase, sentence, or paragraph. How would you get a computer to do it?

      What step-by-step process would you use that would allow a computer to identify how many words there are in a text? Here's one way.

    • Identifying Anagrams

      Identifying Anagram

      Two words or sequences of letters are anagrams of each other if they have the same quantity of the same letters in each word: rat is an anagram of art, and peat is an anagram of tape.

      What are some strategies that you can think of that would help you identify two anagrams? It may help you clarify your strategy if you have an especially long pair of potential anagrams to work on:

      tapbgwbtrkoauetgntcqgbuwotrkrstjnyajnvnfvbazmrtxon

      nqxpytvbtnzbmktuanojwtcekbfrotnguwajtrrvtsgoanrabg

      You could try one of these two strategies.

      Program: magic_8_ball_v2.py

      Write a new version of your Magic 8-Ball program that asks the user if they'd like to ask the 8-Ball a question. If they do, have the user enter their question and provide a reply.   8. Programs:
    1. number_guessing_game.py

      Program: number_guessing_game.py

      This program makes up a secret (random) number between 1 and 10, and gives the user 3 tries to guess it. If the user hasn't guessed after three tries, then number is revealed.

    2. random_walker1.py

      Program: random_walker1.py

      This program models a "random walker" that starts at location 0,0, an intersection in the middle of a city laid out in square blocks. Each turn, the random walker moves one block to the north, east, west, or south, with the x-coordinate changing by 1 block or the y-coordinate changing by one block.

      Print out the move number and the location of the random walker after every turn, and stop the walker when she has returned to the 0,0 starting position.
